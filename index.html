ABOUT US

Formerly known as J&D Stoneworks before the owners parted ways, Vision Stoneworks continues to bring you 40+ years of industry experience. From the young age of 17, John was taken under the wing of an Italian master craftsman who taught him everything he knows today. Quickly observing his skill in the craft, John quickly excelled and soon enough opened his own fabrication shop. His love for stone was made apparent when he would transform his homes into works of art. It is through his passion that we are taking your personal designs and bringing your visions to life. This way your work will always be one of a kind. We believe the modern luxury involves your VISION, not just a trend that passes and so we look forward to working by your side through this transformative process.

Owner | John Kokkinis

Coming from the land of ancient ruins and some of the most famous stone quarries in the world, it is no coincidence where his love for the stone industry began. At a young age he made his way to Canada from the island of Crete seeking new opportunities. Nowadays, those who don’t know him by name likely know of his work including luxury homes to hospitably establishments. As a master craftsman with over 40+ years in the industry you can easily say he is the best at what he does.

Operations Manager | Anastasia Kokkinis

An eye for detail and all things design, it was just a matter of time before Anastasia knew she would join her family business. Starting with the entire rebranding of the company, Anastasia has started to make her imprint. From a young age she remembers her childhood homes consisting of the work of her father. I remember my dad always asking me for my input on which material I liked best and him actually going through with it. At the age of 10 I designed my first marble coffee table in the shape of the Greek evil eye with engraved details.

Both marble and granite are natural and quarried from the earth, but they have many different properties. The biggest difference lies in their porosity. Marble is softer and more porous than granite. Granite, on the other hand, has a crystalline structure that makes it more resistant to abrasions, stains, and discolorations. It’s actually the strongest of all natural stones. Marble has veining throughout, whereas granite features a more fleck-like, granular appearance.
